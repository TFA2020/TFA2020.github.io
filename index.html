"We speak your language"

Quality of Life Issue

Many NYC immigrants are not aware of their rights and benefits. This allows local healthcare facilities to take advantage of a patient and continue their workflow without getting a professional interpreter involved. Our data revealed that 57% of immigrants might not be aware of their rights. This issue affects about 3 in every 5 immigrants.

Know What To Do

If a healthcare provider or their staff members reject an immigrant’s request for translation services, this should be reported to the NYC Commission on Human Rights or Office of Civil Rights (HHS) as a complaint for violation of Title VI of the Civil Rights Act of 1964. Upon reporting of such discrimination, the department of Health will investigate the issue.

Know Your Benefits

If you have Medicaid or Medicare or any form of health insurance funded by the federal government, you have the right to request for professional translation services free of charge when experiencing communication difficulties at a healthcare facility, provided that the facility accepts your health insurance.

The Law Protects You

Title VI of the Civil Rights Act of 1964 "prohibits discrimination on the basis of race, color, and national origin in programs and activities receiving federal financial assistance". President John F. Kennedy once said, "Simple justice requires that public funds, to which all taxpayers of all races [colors, and national origins] contribute, not be spent in any fashion which encourages, entrenches, subsidizes or results in racial [color or national origin] discrimination".
Source : https://www.justice.gov/crt/fcs/TitleVI

Our Recommendation

We plan to improve the quality of life of our chosen population through the suggestion of a new policy that would penalize hospitals for not providing services and conducting an awareness campaign to get our population to be aware of their rights. Our policy would aim to incentivize the offering and implementation of translation services in hospitals under the threat of a fine from the city.
